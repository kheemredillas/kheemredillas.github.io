Introduction

Storage Management refers to the process of efficiently storing, organizing, and protecting digital data.

Redundancy means creating backup or duplicate copies of data to ensure availability and reliability.

  • Understand RAID levels and their functions.
  • Learn about Logical Volume Management (LVM).
  • Compare NAS and SAN storage systems.

What is Storage Management & Redundancy?

Storage management ensures efficiency, reliability, and accessibility of stored data.

Redundancy prevents data loss during system failure through hardware or software duplication.

RAID: Redundant Array of Independent Disks

  • RAID 0: Speed, no redundancy.
  • RAID 1: Mirroring for safety.
  • RAID 5: Striping + Parity for balance.
  • RAID 10: Mirroring + Striping for high performance and protection.

LVM (Logical Volume Management)

Allows dynamic resizing of partitions with components like PV (Physical Volume), VG (Volume Group), and LV (Logical Volume).

Provides flexibility, scalability, and easier backups.

Analysis / Implications

  • Improved data security and uptime.
  • Efficient data management and scalability.
  • Challenges: cost and complexity.

Conclusion: Balancing performance, reliability, and cost is key.

Conclusion

Storage management ensures data is organized, protected, and accessible. Redundancy safeguards against failures and data loss.

RAID, LVM, NAS, and SAN are key technologies in modern systems.
